Module 4 · Activity 1 of 3

Your First Instinct

⏱ About 3 minutes

Before you read about design thinking, notice your own default approach to problems. There's no right or wrong answer here — the goal is self-awareness.

When you encounter a problem, what do you usually do first?
Do you jump to solutions? Research first? Talk to others? Avoid it? Sit with it?
Has this instinct ever led you to solve the wrong problem — or miss something important?
💡 Noticing your instinct is the first step to expanding it. Design thinking doesn't replace how you think — it adds new options.

Module 4 · Activity 3 of 3

Rough Prototype & Reflection

⏱ About 8–10 minutes

Take one of your two top ideas and make it rough and real. A prototype doesn't have to be built — it can be a sketch, a storyboard, a one-paragraph description, or a role play scenario. The goal is to make the idea tangible enough to ask questions about it.

Which idea are you prototyping?
Describe your prototype — what format did you choose and what does it show?
A sketch, storyboard, one-pager, or description of a scenario all count
What is the one thing you most want to learn by testing this?
A good prototype answers a specific question — not all questions
🔍 Reflection
Did you find it easy or hard to generate ideas without judging them — what got in the way?
Which of your ideas surprised you — and why?
