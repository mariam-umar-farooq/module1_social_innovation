Have You Already Innovated?

⏱ About 3–5 minutes

Before you read about social innovation, think about whether you've already experienced it. It doesn't have to be big — a creative solution to a problem affecting others, in any context, counts.

💡 Think of a time when someone found a new, creative way to solve a problem that was affecting other people.
It could be something in your neighbourhood, university, family, or workplace — anything counts

Module 2 · Activity 3 of 3

Your First Social Innovation Idea

⏱ About 10–12 minutes

Go back to the social challenge you identified in Module 1. Using what you've just learned, sketch your first rough idea for a social innovation response. This is not a plan — it's a creative first step. You'll refine it in the modules ahead.

What is the social need you're addressing?
What is your new approach or idea?
Who would benefit — and how?
Who would you need to work with?
How is this different from what already exists?
🔍 Reflection
🤔 Does your idea feel "big enough" to count as social innovation — and why do you think that?
Most first ideas feel too small. That's normal. Notice the instinct — and question it.
🧠 What assumptions are you making about who has the knowledge to solve this problem?
Are you assuming the solution comes from experts, outsiders, or the community itself?
💬 Peer prompt: Share your rough idea with one other person and ask: "What's one thing I might be missing?" Note their response below if you'd like.
